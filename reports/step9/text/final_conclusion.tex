In the block-adjusted mixed-effects analysis, the abstract-versus-concrete effect in the left frontal ROI was reduced and was not statistically significant after controlling for global block order, within-block trial position, participant condition order, question length, and item difficulty (beta=3.020e-08, SE=2.276e-08, p=0.184407, 95% CI [-1.440e-08, 7.481e-08]). This suggests that the residual frontal tendency does not survive clear statistical support once block structure is modeled explicitly. None of the predeclared condition-by-block interaction terms reached the alpha=0.05 threshold.
